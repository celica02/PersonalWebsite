Save The Chicken - 2017

Final project of the first year of the Video Game Development degree at UCM. Developed in Unity by a group of 4 students.

It's a 2D platform infity runner. The protagonist of the game is a chicken who is running away from the farm because she doesn't feel like becoming the dinner of the day. On her escape to the "Chicken Eden" she has to pass through several areas and survive obstacles and enemies. To make the race easier for her, she will encounter various power ups that will give her various powers for a period of time.



Edelweiss - 2018

Final project of the second year of the Video Game Development degree at UCM. Developed in C++ with SDL in a group of 8 students. Together we designed and developed the game.

It's a survival horror in which the protagonist wakes up, confused and unable to remember anything, in an abandoned hospital overwhelmed by monsters. She will have to collect materials to fight and survive through the experience, and discover what has happened.

Chis - 2019

Final project of the third year of the Video Game Development degree at UCM. Developed in C++, the goal of the project was to build our own engine and make a game with it. Ogre, PhysX, FMOD and CEGUI were used to do so, and it was developed by 5 students of the degree.

A first-person shooter where you have to eliminate the enemies (rats) from the map. The player has been downsized and the battle takes place on their cluttered dining table.

Final Degree Project - 2020/2021

Final project for the UCM's Video Game Development degree.

It's a mobile application to facilitate recycling. Using the device's camera, it detects the material of the object being focused on and reports the result through the interface, showing the percentage of accuracy of the identified material.

In addition to the app, it was investigated the use of synthetic images to train neural networks. To do so, several captures of three-dimensional models were taken, changing their position, rotation and background on each one. Then, they were used to train the neural network with different proportions of synthetic and real images. Finally, the training results were compared and it was concluded that having a dataset of images where 30% were real and the remaining 70% were synthetically generated, the neural network detected the materials with 93% accuracy.

To accomplish all of this, it was created an application using Unity, which automatically generated the synthetic images. Then, using these images and mixing them with real ones, the neural network was trained in Python using Tensorflow Lite. Once the network had been trained, the resulting data was introduced into the mobile application, developed in Android Studio.

Spooky Hat Shop - 2021/2022

Final project for the Master's Degree in Video Game Development at the UCM.

Developed in Unreal Engine by a multidisciplinary team bringing together students from different masters. It was mainly used C++ together with Blueprints.

The goal is to deliver requested hats to the customers before the time runs out. To do so, the player has to go through the warehouse, a procedurally generated dungeon full of obstacles which contains the materials needed for the hats. Once everything needed has been collected, the hats can be crafted and finally delivered.
